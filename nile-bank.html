Manage Finances

Brief

Nile Bank is known for providing reliable banking solutions, but younger users wanted more. They needed tools that went beyond basic payments, helping them manage their money day-to-day. To meet this need, we developed the Surplus Manager a feature that helps young professionals stay on top of their finances, manage their paychecks , and reach their savings goals .

Starting thoughts…

Young people today demand more than basic banking. They want smart tools that help them budget , save , and grow . With traditional banks not keeping up, Nile Bank aimed to change that by creating a feature that fits seamlessly into their users' lives, especially for those juggling multiple financial responsibilities.

Solution

Surplus Manager

Surplus Manager simplifies financial management . Whether it's tracking your spending , setting savings goals , or managing upcoming bills , this feature puts everything in one place. Users can organize their finances around their paycheck schedule, making it easier to plan and control their money.

Manage Finances Easily

Managing your money shouldn’t feel overwhelming. That’s why Nile Bank provides you with a seamless experience to help you stay on top of your finances, whether you're monitoring your surplus, tracking expenses, or ensuring bills are paid on time. With just a few taps, you can understand your financial standing and make smarter choices for your future.

Manage Your Budget

gives a quick overview of your surplus balance and recent spending. With clear summaries and simple navigation, you can stay on top of your finances at a glance.

Build a Financial Future

screen offers insight into your financial habits over time. The clean graphs show your open balances and savings month by month, helping you plan better for the future.

Easy Bill Pay

Easily handle Your bills. See payee, amount, due date at a glance. Use autopay or manual pay in the app. Stay organized, avoid late fees, prioritize your finances.

01.
DISCOVER

How can simplify financial management for young Egyptian professionals?

Secondary Research

The global financial technology (fintech) market is expanding rapidly, and Egypt’s growth is equally promising. Yet, managing personal finances can be overwhelming for consumers, especially with the growing number of financial management tools. Many banking apps offer standard features but lack meaningful guidance for users to make informed financial decisions.

Screener Survey

After analyzing global and Egyptian fintech trends, I conducted a screener survey to gauge how Egyptian consumers manage their finances. The survey revealed that most users feel overwhelmed by manual calculations and managing multiple apps to handle their finances. They expressed a strong need for a comprehensive app that simplifies financial management and integrates various tools such as bill payments, savings tracking, and spending insights.

User Interview

From the survey, I selected participants for deeper interviews. These users often relied on multiple banking apps for different tasks but felt frustrated with the lack of integration and user-friendly tools. They expressed a desire for an app that combines essential features such as automated bill pay, surplus tracking, and savings management in one place.

In-Person & Self-Guided Treatment

Success Factors

  • In-Person

  • Access to branch managers for personalized guidance and expert advice when needed.

  • Self-Guided

  • Ease of monitoring balances, savings, and expenses through a simplified app interface

Failure Factors

  • In-Person

  • Time-consuming visits to branches and the hassle of managing finances manually.

  • Self-Guided

  • Lack of motivation and understanding of how to best use surplus income for saving or investing.

Expert Interview

To validate user pain points and needs, I interviewed financial advisors who emphasized the importance of simplifying personal financial management tools. They highlighted that users often struggle with tracking expenses, missing payment deadlines, and calculating surpluses manually.

Expert Insight

  • Misconception of Users: Many users misunderstand how to balance paying off debt while saving.

  • Professional Guidance: Financial advisors suggest using tools to split extra money between savings and paying off debt.

  • Improvement Suggestion: Experts suggested including a goal-setting feature that guides users to allocate their surplus toward both emergency savings and debt repayment based on their unique financial situation.

Competitor Analysis

I began by analyzing both direct and indirect competitors in the financial management and digital banking space, focusing on apps that offer automated bill payment, expense tracking, and surplus management features. This analysis helped me identify key opportunities to enhance Nile Bank’s user experience by integrating features that address gaps in financial planning, automation, and surplus tracking.

IDEATE

How can we simplify financial management for young professionals in Egypt?

Problem Statements

After analyzing Laila’s journey, I identified three main challenges:

  1. How might we help users better track their spending to improve their financial health?
  2. How might we help users set and achieve realistic savings goals?
  3. How might we simplify bill management for users while keeping them in control?

I used the Crazy 8s brainstorming method to explore ideas for paycheck management, savings tracking, and bill payment. This helped identify the core functionalities for Surplus Manager, such as personalized savings trackers and streamlined bill payment tools.

04.
PROTOTYPE

Creating an intuitive user experience

Wireframes & User Flow

Based on these ideas, I designed wireframes around the Surplus Manager and core financial management features. These wireframes allow users to track their financial surplus, schedule bill payments, and manage their credit cards all from one central hub. I ensured the user flow was intuitive from the moment they logged in to when they viewed their savings or paid a bill.

Low Fidelity Prototype

I created a low-fidelity prototype to test the initial design of the Surplus Manager feature. Users appreciated the organized structure but provided feedback on improving the clarity of the summary page and the autopay management process.

High Fidelity Prototype

The high-fidelity prototype explored multiple design options, allowing me to test and refine the overall experience. Users could now navigate seamlessly between managing bills, tracking their surplus, and understanding their spending patterns. This feedback helped in fine-tuning the final design and features.

Sticker Sheet

A sticker sheet was developed to ensure visual consistency across the app. This included color schemes, typography, and reusable components, ensuring that each element aligned with the brand and user expectations.

Usability Testing

After conducting usability tests with 4 participants, I gathered insights to refine the final design. Users appreciated the Surplus Manager's intuitive interface and the overall ease of use. Based on the feedback, I designed the Surplus Manager Outgoing screen to provide a clearer overview of upcoming and completed payments, ensuring users could manage their outgoing transactions seamlessly.

05.
REFLECTION

Improving the app based on user feedback

Key Insights

  • Users prioritized the Surplus Manager with Bill Pay and Credit Card Management as helpful features.
  • The Automated Bill Pay feature was highly appreciated for simplifying financial obligations.
  • Users valued clear visuals for surplus and savings but wanted a stronger focus on Financial Management Tools.

Final Revisions

Based on feedback, I made several key revisions:

  • Enhanced the Surplus Manager to show spending insights and surplus trends at a glance.
  • Simplified the Credit Card Management and Bill Pay flows to ensure users can schedule payments quickly.
  • Focused on delivering a clean, intuitive interface that allows users to manage their finances effortlessly without unnecessary distractions.

What I Learned

Building the Nile Bank app taught me the importance of balancing an intuitive user interface with powerful financial management tools. By listening to user feedback, I was able to refine the app to ensure it met the core financial needs of young professionals while making financial management easier and more accessible.

Next Steps

In the next phase, I will continue testing and refining the Surplus Manager to ensure it delivers the best possible experience for users. The goal is to launch an MVP that focuses on core features like surplus tracking, automated bill pay, and credit card management. Future updates will include additional features such as investment insights and goal tracking.
